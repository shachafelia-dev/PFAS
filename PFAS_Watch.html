🔬 CCR vs UCMR5 Data

  • CCR PFAS ranges = min/max across all sampling points over the year
  • UCMR5 = standardized EPA method, specific entry-point sampling
  • CCR "ND" = Non-Detect (below lab reporting limit)
  • CCR "NA" = Not Applicable / system did not test / not required
  • Both sources use same EPA MCL thresholds (PFOA/PFOS = 4 ng/L)

❓ Why 17 Utilities Not in UCMR5?

  • 13 systems: <3,300 people — not randomly selected
  • 2 systems: 3,300–10,000 — not notified by EPA
  • 2 systems: newly acquired 2025 — post-UCMR5 cycle
🔬 Data Collection Methodology

📋 Overview

This dashboard combines data from three independent sources. Each source uses a different collection methodology, and results are not directly cross-comparable between sources. UCMR5 charts are generated directly from the EPA CSV file.

① EPA UCMR5 Dataset

  • Coverage: 19 of 36 NJAW utilities
  • Period: January 2023 – December 2025
  • File: NJ_American_Water_utilities_UCMR5_all.csv
  • Unit: Raw values in µg/L → converted to ng/L (×1000)
  • Non-detects: Rows with sign="<" excluded from charts
  • Replicates: Same entry point + same date + different values → averaged (shown as ★)
  • Reliability: Highest — fully standardized, lab-verified

② CCR 2024 Water Quality Reports

  • Coverage: 17 of 36 NJAW utilities (not in UCMR5)
  • Period: Full year 2024
  • Who collects: Utility self-reported annual CCR
  • Method: Values shown as min–max range across all samples
  • Reliability: Moderate — self-reported, ranges not directly comparable to UCMR5
Primary Sources: EPA UCMR5 Data Finder (epa.gov/dwucmr)  ·  NJ WaterCheck (njwatercheck.com)  ·  NJ American Water CCR PDFs (amwater.com/ccr)  ·  NJDEP Drinking Water Watch (dep.nj.gov/watersupply)
