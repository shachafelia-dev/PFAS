🔬 CCR vs UCMR5 Data

  • CCR PFAS ranges = min/max across all sampling points over the year
  • UCMR5 = standardized EPA method, specific entry-point sampling
  • CCR "ND" = Non-Detect (below lab reporting limit)
  • CCR "NA" = Not Applicable / system did not test / not required
  • Both sources use same EPA MCL thresholds (PFOA/PFOS = 4 ng/L)

🔬 Data Collection Methodology

📋 Overview

This dashboard combines data from three independent sources to provide a complete picture of PFAS contamination across all 36 NJ American Water utilities. Each source uses a different collection methodology, and results are not directly cross-comparable between sources.

① EPA UCMR5 Dataset

  • Coverage: 19 of 36 NJAW utilities
  • Period: January 2023 – December 2025
  • Who collects: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), federally mandated
  • Method: Standardized sampling at entry points to distribution system (EPTDS); EPA-approved labs only
  • Contaminants: 29 PFAS compounds + lithium
  • Applicability: Mandatory for systems serving >10,000 people; 3,300–10,000 if notified; 800 randomly selected small systems
  • Reliability: Highest — fully standardized, lab-verified, independently audited

② CCR 2024 Water Quality Reports

  • Coverage: 17 of 36 NJAW utilities (not in UCMR5)
  • Period: Full year 2024
  • Who collects: Utility self-reported; published in annual Consumer Confidence Report (CCR)
  • Method: Utility-run sampling across multiple entry points; values shown as min–max range across all samples
  • Contaminants: PFOA, PFOS, PFNA primarily
  • Applicability: Required to publish results for contaminants tested; not all small systems test for PFAS
  • Reliability: Moderate — self-reported, ranges not directly comparable to UCMR5 point measurements
👥 Population Data Methodology

Method 1 — NJ WaterCheck (Exact)

Primary source for all systems that have a dedicated page on njwatercheck.com. The "Est. Population Served" field is pulled directly from NJDEP data and displayed on each system's detail page.

Systems confirmed this way:

  • Belvidere → 2,644
  • New Egypt → 704
  • Homestead → 1,765
  • Twin Lakes → 120
  • Vincentown → 132
  • Egg Harbor City → 3,180
  • South Orange Village → 16,587
  • Penns Grove → 10,843
  • Roxbury → 14,135
  • Shorelands → 31,908
  • … and all large UCMR5 systems

Method 2 — CCR PDF Daily Usage (Estimated)

For systems not on NJ WaterCheck, each utility's official CCR PDF was fetched from amwater.com/ccr/[system].pdf. The "Quick Facts" section of every CCR lists:

  • Communities served
  • Average daily water supplied (MGD)

Population is then estimated using the standard EPA per-capita consumption rate:

Pop ≈ Daily Usage (gallons) ÷ 100 gpcd
Example: 0.16 MGD ÷ 100 = ~1,600 people

Systems estimated this way are marked ~ in all tables.

Method 3 — Municipality / Census Context

For a small number of systems where neither NJ WaterCheck nor CCR MGD data was available, population was estimated by cross-referencing the communities served listed in the CCR against NJ municipal census data.

Applied to: Salem, Shrewsbury-AVMA, Harrison, Cape May Court House, Ocean City.

These figures are approximate and also marked ~.

PWSID Confirmation

Every PWSID (Public Water System ID) was confirmed directly from the official CCR PDF cover page for each utility:

Source: amwater.com/ccr/[system].pdf
Field: "PWS ID: NJ_______"
Printed on cover of each annual CCR report

Cross-verified against NJDEP Drinking Water Watch and NJ WaterCheck system pages where available.

⚠ Limitations & Caveats
  • CCR vs UCMR5: CCR PFAS values are ranges (min–max) across all sampling points for the full year. UCMR5 values are single-point measurements at entry points. They use different sampling protocols and cannot be directly compared.
  • "No data" systems: 7 utilities (Deep Run, Egg Harbor City, Homestead, New Egypt, Strathmere, Sunbury, Vincentown) did not include any PFAS data in their 2024 CCR. This may mean the system was exempt, testing was not completed, or results are pending.
  • Population estimates (~ marked): Derived from daily water usage ÷ 100 gpcd. Actual population may differ based on commercial/industrial usage, seasonal variation, or purchased water ratios.
  • Newly acquired systems: South Orange Village (Mar 2025) and Shrewsbury-AVMA (Apr 2025) were acquired after the UCMR5 window. Their CCR data comes from the prior owner's 2024 report, posted by NJAW.
  • MCL effective date: EPA PFAS MCLs (PFOA/PFOS = 4 ng/L) were finalized April 2024. Compliance required by 2026. All comparisons in this dashboard use the 2024 MCL values.
