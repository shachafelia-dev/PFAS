❓ What is PFAS?

Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) are synthetic chemicals used since the 1940s in industrial and consumer products. Known as "forever chemicals," they persist indefinitely in the environment and bioaccumulate in humans. Health effects include increased cancer risk, thyroid disruption, immune suppression, and reproductive harm. The EPA established enforceable MCLs for key PFAS in 2024, with compliance required by 2026.
